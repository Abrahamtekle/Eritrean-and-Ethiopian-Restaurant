✦ How It Began

Food From the Heart

Our restaurant was born from a simple belief: that the bold, vibrant food of Eritrea and Ethiopia deserves a home in Calgary. We opened our doors to share the flavours of East Africa with this incredible city — and the response has been overwhelming.

Every dish on our menu tells a story. The injera, fermented for days. The berbere, ground fresh from whole spices. The slow-cooked wats that fill the kitchen with the scent of home. We cook the way our families taught us — with patience, love, and no shortcuts.

Whether you're Ethiopian, Eritrean, or completely new to East African food, you are welcome at our table. That's the spirit of this cuisine — sharing, community, and joy.

✦ What Drives Us

Our Mission & Vision

Two values guide us every day: authentic flavour and genuine community.

Our Mission

To serve the most authentic Eritrean and Ethiopian cuisine in Calgary — made from scratch, every day, with traditional spices and time-honoured techniques. We want every guest to taste the real culture of East Africa in every bite.

Our Vision

To become Calgary's most beloved cultural dining destination — a place where people of all backgrounds discover and fall in love with East African food, and where the Eritrean and Ethiopian community feels truly at home.

✦ The People Behind It

Meet the Team

Passionate cooks and hosts who put their hearts into every plate.

Abraham Tekle
Founder & Head Chef

Born in Eritrea and raised with a deep love of traditional cooking. Abraham brings decades of family recipes and culinary passion to every dish he creates in Calgary.

Miriam Haile
Head Cook – Ethiopian

Miriam specializes in classic Ethiopian stews and vegetarian dishes. Her Doro Wat and Misir Wat are the most ordered dishes on our menu.

Yonas Berhe
Eritrean Cuisine Specialist

Yonas grew up in Asmara and brings authentic Eritrean flavours to Calgary. His Zigni and Shiro recipes are rooted in generations of tradition.

Tigist Alemu
Pastry & Beverages

Tigist handles our traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremonies and honey wine (Tej). Her pastries and sambusa are Calgary's best-kept secret.
